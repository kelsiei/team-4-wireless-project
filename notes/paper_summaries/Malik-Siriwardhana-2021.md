  ## Security and Privacy in Artificial Intelligence-Enabled 6G

### Citation
Y. Siriwardhana, P. Porambage, M. Liyanage, and M. Ylianttila, “AI and 6G Security: Opportunities and Challenges,” in 2021 Joint European Conference on Networks and Communications & 6G Summit (EuCNC/6G Summit), 2021, pp. 616–621, doi: 10.1109/EuCNC/6GSummit51104.2021.9482503.

  ## Summary

This paper explains how artificial intelligence could become a major part of security in future 6G networks. The authors argue that 6G will rely more on automation, intelligent orchestration, and adaptive management than earlier wireless generations, which means AI can help with proactive threat discovery and intelligent mitigation. At the same time, the paper shows that AI also creates new security risks and challenges that must be addressed carefully.

### Relevance to the Research Topic

This paper is useful for our project because it provides the broader motivation for studying AI security in 6G. It helps explain why traditional security methods alone may not be enough in future autonomous networks and why AI-driven defenses will become more important as network complexity increases.

### Critical Comment

This paper is strong as an overview, but it stays at a high level. It explains the opportunities and challenges clearly, but it does not test one detailed defense method in depth.
