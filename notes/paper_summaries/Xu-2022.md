  ## Security-and-Privacy-in-AI-Enabled-6G.md

### Citation
Q. Xu, Z. Su, and R. Li, “Security and Privacy in Artificial Intelligence-Enabled 6G,” IEEE Network, vol. 36, no. 5, pp. 188–196, 2022, doi: 10.1109/MNET.117.2100730.

  ## Summary

This paper studies security and privacy in AI-enabled 6G and explains that federated learning will likely play an important role in training models across distributed devices without always sharing raw data. The authors show that while this can improve efficiency and privacy, it also introduces risks such as cheating attacks, low-quality local training, and privacy leakage during the learning process. The paper also discusses protection ideas including trust evaluation, incentive mechanisms, and local differential privacy.

### Relevance to the Research Topic

This source is highly relevant to our project because it shows that 6G security is not only about protecting communication channels. It also requires protecting the AI systems and distributed learning processes that future networks will depend on. This makes the paper useful for explaining how AI expands the 6G attack surface.

### Critical Comment

A limitation of this paper is that it is more conceptual than experimental. It presents useful threats and protection ideas, but it does not fully prove how well these methods would work in a realistic large-scale 6G environment.
