# Paper Summary - Artificial intelligence in zero trust architecture for 5G and 6G networks: A systematic literature review

**Citation:** [Ali, S. M. M. (2025). Artificial intelligence in zero trust architecture for 5G and 6G networks: A systematic literature review. Jamk University of Applied Sciences https://www.theseus.fi/bitstream/10024/908381/2/Ali_Syed_Muhammad_Muneeb.pdf]

## Summary

This paper is about how AI is needed to make Zero Trust security work in 5G and 6G networks. The author looks at existing studies and found that the old ways of doing security just can not handle how big and fast networks are getting nowadays. One thing that AI did good was that it cut down false positives from 9.3% to 1.8%. The main problem the paper points out is that all this AI stuff adds extra processing time which is an issue since 6G is supposed to be super fast.


## Relevance

This paper is related to our topic since its about using AI to defend 6G networks from security threats. It covers a lot of the specific things we are looking at like how AI detects anomalies and handles access control in these next gen networks. It also talks about the kinds of attacks that make 6G security so difficult, which helps give context to why AI defenses are even necessary in the first place.

## Critical Comment

The biggest limitation is that everything is based on exisitng studies and simulations since no actual 6G networks was tested, so its hard to know if any of it would work. A lot of the numbers like the false positive rates come from controlled experiments which dont reflect how messy real networks actually are. It also briefly mentions adversarial attacks on the AI itself without really getting into how that would be handled.
