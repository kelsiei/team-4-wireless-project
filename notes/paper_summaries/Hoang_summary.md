# Paper Summary - Security risks and countermeasures of adversarial attacks on AI-driven applications in 6G networks: A survey
  
  Citation: V.-T. Hoang, Y. A. Ergu, V.-L. Nguyen, and R.-G. Chang, “Security risks and countermeasures of adversarial attacks on AI-driven applications in 6G networks: A survey,” Journal of Network and Computer Applications, vol. 232, p. 104031, Dec. 2024, doi: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jnca.2024.104031.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
  ## Summary

This survey looks at how adversarial attacks threaten AI systems in 6G networks, covering a range of network types and functions. It categorizes attacks by how much information the attacker has, what they're trying to achieve, and what method they use. The paper examines specific attack techniques and shows how they could disrupt key 6G operations like managing radio resources and estimating channel conditions. It also reviews defense strategies, including adversarial training and the potential use of quantum technologies to detect unusual network behavior.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                  

  ## Relevance to our Survey

The survey helps the team pinpoint specific vulnerabilities, such as how sophisticated attackers might try to get around Zero Trust protections. This source acts as a guide for building the kind of layered defense system the project needs to protect 6G networks.                                                                                                                                                                      



  ## An Issue I Identified

Right now, adversarial attack research is mostly focused on computer vision, with only limited progress in areas like NLP and speech recognition. Expanding this research into other domains is important as many existing attack methods could also be adapted to areas relevant to 6G.
