  ## System-level-Analysis-of-Adversarial-Attacks-and-Defenses-on-Intelligence-in-O-RAN-based-Cellular-Networks.md

### Citation
A. Chiejina, B. Kim, K. Chowdhury, and V. K. Shah, “System-level Analysis of Adversarial Attacks and Defenses on Intelligence in O-RAN based Cellular Networks,” arXiv:2402.06846, 2024.

  ## Summary

This paper studies adversarial attacks against machine-learning-based xApps in O-RAN cellular systems. The authors show that these attacks can seriously reduce model accuracy and disrupt intelligent radio control. They also test a defense based on distillation and show that it performs better than standard adversarial training under the timing limits of near-real-time network control.

### Relevance to the Research Topic

This paper is very relevant to our project because it gives a concrete example of adversarial machine learning in a network architecture related to future 6G systems. It helps show that AI security risks in 6G are not only theoretical and that practical defenses must work under real timing constraints.

### Critical Comment

A limitation of this paper is that it focuses on one O-RAN setting and a limited set of models. Because of that, the results are useful and practical, but they may not apply equally to every 6G environment.
