  ## Security-and-Privacy-in-AI-Enabled-6G.md

### Citation
Q. Xu, Z. Su, and R. Li, “Security and Privacy in Artificial Intelligence-Enabled 6G,” IEEE Network, vol. 36, no. 5, pp. 188–196, 2022, doi: 10.1109/MNET.117.2100730.

  ## Summary

This paper explains that AI will be a core part of 6G networks and that federated learning will likely be used to train models across many devices without always sharing raw data. The paper shows that this approach improves efficiency and privacy in theory, but it also introduces new risks such as cheating attacks, weak local training, and privacy leakage during the learning process.

### Relevance to the Research Topic

This paper is useful for our project because it shows that in 6G, security is not only about protecting communication links. It also becomes necessary to protect the AI training process itself, especially when learning is distributed across many devices and systems.

### Critical Comment

A limitation of this paper is that it is more conceptual than experimental. It presents useful threats and protection ideas, but it does not fully prove how well these methods would work in a realistic large-scale 6G environment.
