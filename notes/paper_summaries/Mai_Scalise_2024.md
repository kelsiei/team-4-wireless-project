# Paper Summary - A Systematic Survey on 5G and 6G Security Considerations, Challenges, Trends, and Research Areas

**Citation:** [Scalise, P.; Boeding, M.; Hempel, M.; Sharif, H.; Delloiacovo, J.; Reed, J. (2024). A Systematic Survey on 5G and 6G Security Considerations, Challenges, Trends, and Research Areas. Future Internet, 16(3), 67. https://doi.org/10.3390/fi16030067]

## Summary

This paper covers pretty much everything about 5G security from the Core Network to the Radio Access Network (RAN) to User Equipment. The author breaks down security into three models that are confidentality, integrity and availability and then looks at where 5G still has problems like the 5G-AKA authentication protocol which lets attackers track users. They also spend a lot of time talking about Zero Trust Architecture and future stuff like post quantum cryptography and blockchain for 6G. 

## Relevance

This is directly relevant to our topic because it lays out the current threat landscape that 6G will herit from 5G. It covers network slicing attacks, eavesdropping on MIMO and RIS systems and vulnerabilities in SDN and SFV. It even mentions adversarial machine learning attacks which is exactly the kind of threat we are looking at.

## Critical Comment

This paper is a survey so it does not propose any new solutions itself, it just summarizes other people's work. It means you have to go read the actual papers if you want details on everything. Also the AI security section is pretty short compared to how much space they give blockchain and PQC.
